# Call of Duty: Black Ops 2 (Zombies) Inspiration

> "i think at that age it was call of duty black ops 2. that also touched on flyff aoeing!! but back then it was called "rape training". well call of duty was definitely a mature game with a mature community! regardless, it was fun to gather up many zombies and then shoot through them with a variety of standard military weapons and equipment like pistols, submachine guns, LMG, snipers. and then of course getting 2 grenades per round and upgrading them to semtex sticky grenades felt good. of course it was scarier and the maps were far more detailed than flyff. but the essential game mechanic remains satisfying to this day"

## Core Highlights
*   **The "Training" Mechanic:** Gathering large groups of zombies into a tight pack (historically referred to by the community as "training") to maximize ammo efficiency and safety.
*   **Weapon Variety & Feel:** Satisfaction from a diverse arsenal (Pistols, SMGs, LMGs, Snipers) with distinct handling and "punch."
*   **Tactical Equipment:** Resource management of grenades, and the progression to specialized equipment like Semtex or Monkey Bombs.
*   **Atmospheric Tension:** High-detail, claustrophobic, and scary environments that contrast with the more open "grind" of games like FlyFF.
*   **Penetration Mechanics:** The satisfaction of a single bullet or explosive clearing a line of enemies within a "train."

## Research Findings
*   **AI Pathfinding:** Zombies use a combination of direct tracking and "zoning" to ensure they don't all overlap, which actually makes "training" possible by creating a predictable but dangerous flow.
*   **Wall-Buys vs. Mystery Box:** A dual-layer progression system where players can choose between reliable, fixed-position weapons or gambling for high-power "Wonder Weapons."
*   **Pack-a-Punch:** A definitive "upgrade" milestone that fundamentally changes how weapons behave (damage, ammo capacity, special effects).
